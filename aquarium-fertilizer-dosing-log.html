Macros vs micros

Planted-tank ferts split into two groups. Macros — nitrogen (NO₃), phosphate (PO₄) and potassium (K) — are consumed in larger amounts and drive bulk growth. Micros — iron and the other trace elements — are needed in tiny doses but are just as capable of bottlenecking a tank when missing. Most aquascapers dose them on alternating days so the two don't react in the water column.

EI, lean, or somewhere between

The Estimative Index (EI) deliberately doses nutrients in excess and relies on a large weekly water change (~50%) to reset levels — simple and forgiving for high-light, CO₂-injected tanks. Lean dosing aims to supply just enough, which suits low-tech setups and sensitive shrimp. Neither is "correct"; the right amount depends on your light, CO₂ and plant mass. The only way to find your sweet spot is to log what you dose and check it against your water tests.
